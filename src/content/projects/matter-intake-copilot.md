## The problem

New matter intake is slow, inconsistent and full of re-keying. A single enquiry can pass through three people before it becomes a usable file, and the structured data that downstream systems need is often captured last, badly, or not at all.

## The approach

A focused assistant that sits at the very front of the process. It reads the inbound enquiry, drafts a structured matter summary, flags likely conflicts for a human to confirm, and proposes the right team and rate basis. A person stays in the loop for every decision - the tool does the assembly, not the judgement.

## The outcome

Early testing points to a meaningful reduction in time-to-open and far more consistent structured data from day one. The bigger win is qualitative: fee-earners start with a clean, complete file instead of a blank form.

## What I learned

The hard part was never the model. It was deciding exactly where the human belongs in the loop, and designing the handoffs so the tool feels like leverage rather than another system to feed.
